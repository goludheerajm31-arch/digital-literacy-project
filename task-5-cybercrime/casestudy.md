# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers pretend to be a trusted organization or person to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or OTPs. It is usually carried out through fake emails, messages, or websites that look legitimate.

## How the Crime Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The attacker creates a fake email or message that appears to come from a bank, online service, or popular company.
2. The message creates urgency, such as claiming account suspension or suspicious activity.
3. A malicious link is included that redirects the victim to a fake website designed to look real.
4. The victim enters login credentials, card details, or OTP information.
5. The attacker collects this data and uses it to access accounts or perform financial fraud.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks commonly target students, working professionals, elderly users, and people unfamiliar with cybersecurity practices. Users who frequently use online banking or shopping platforms are especially vulnerable.

## Consequences

Victims may lose money, have their personal data stolen, or experience unauthorized access to social media and banking accounts. Organizations may also suffer reputational damage and financial losses. In severe cases, stolen identities can be used for further cybercrimes.

## Example Incident

In 2020, several users reported phishing emails pretending to be from well-known banks in India, leading to unauthorized UPI transactions after victims shared OTP details. This highlights the importance of verifying links and never sharing confidential information online.

## Prevention Tips

- Never click suspicious links.
- Check sender email addresses carefully.
- Enable two-factor authentication.
- Do not share OTPs or passwords with anyone.
